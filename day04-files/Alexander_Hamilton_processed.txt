- Born in 1755 on the Caribbean island of Nevis to hardship; father left, mother died young, poverty.  
- Worked in a local trading company as a teen, managing ships and goods.  
- His powerful letters drew local leaders to fund his education in New York.  
- Moved to America, supported the independence movement, studied, and wrote defense essays.  
- Joined the army and became aide-de-camp to General George Washington, handling reports and plans.  
- After the Revolution, he saw chaos: debts, weak government, lack of direction.  
- As the first Secretary of the Treasury, built the United States’ financial system: national bank, debt management, strong federal money control.  
- Helped transform the country from a collection of states into a unified economic power.  
- Fought with enemies like Thomas Jefferson over centralized power, helping shape the first political parties.  
- Died in 1804 after a duel with Aaron Burr; Burr’s shot wounded him, and he died the next day.  
- Legacy rests on ideas and policy achievements, not inheritance; started with nothing and shaped a nation through words and will.
