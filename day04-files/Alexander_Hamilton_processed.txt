- Born 1755 on the Caribbean island of Nevis; faced hardship: father left, mother died, poverty.
- As a teen, worked for a trading company; his letters impressed local leaders who funded his education in New York.
- Emigrated to America, supported independence; studied, wrote essays, and joined the army.
- Became aide-de-camp to General George Washington; wrote reports, organized plans, and managed communications.
- After the Revolution, the U.S. faced chaos: debts, weak government, and lack of direction.
- As the first Secretary of the Treasury, built the nation’s financial system: national bank, debt repayment system, and strong federal monetary control.
- His vision helped unify the states into a single economic power, though he faced opposition from rivals like Thomas Jefferson.
- Political battles with Jefferson helped shape the first American political parties.
- Died in 1804 after a duel with Aaron Burr; Burr’s shot killed him (he died the next day).
- Legacy: ideas-driven impact rather than inherited wealth; rose from nothing to help shape a nation.
